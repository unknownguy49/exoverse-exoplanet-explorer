What is an Exoplanet?

Exoplanets, or extrasolar planets, are mesmerizing worlds that exist beyond our solar system, orbiting distant stars in the vast cosmos. Each exoplanet tells a unique story, revealing the diverse conditions that can arise in the universe. From gas giants with swirling storms larger than Earth to rocky planets nestled in the habitable zone where life might flourish, these celestial bodies challenge our understanding of planetary formation and evolution. As we discover more exoplanets through advanced telescopes and innovative techniques, we inch closer to answering one of humanity's greatest questions: are we alone in the universe? Each new finding ignites our imagination and deepens our appreciation for the intricate tapestry of the cosmos.
